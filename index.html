Hacktron Bootcamp

General:

  • You should have to follow all the given instructions and guidelines.
  • It is appreciated if you can attend the sessions 10 minutes before.
  • Participants can ask questions from the resource person through the Slido platform.
  • Kindly remember that harassment, racism or inappropriate behaviour is not allowed during all the sessions.
  • The decision of the judge panel is the final decision. The decisions will be strictly in accordance to the rules and regulations of the Bootcamp.

Bootcamp Contest:

  • Anyone who likes to do Game Development can participate in this Bootcamp.
  • There are four tasks in this boot camp,
    1. Scripting Task
    2. Concept Art & 2D Gaming Task
    3. 3D Modeling and Animation Task
    4. Terrain Designing Task
  • All the above 4 tasks will be open from the 4th of October and the deadline for the completion of the tasks will be on the 20th of October, midnight.
  • You can complete all the four tasks or any number of tasks as you prefer.
  • Participants must score 30% or more from any of these tasks to be eligible to obtain the certificates.
  • The tasks will be given with regard to the content delivered throughout the series of sessions in the Bootcamp.
  • Cash prizes,Certificates and Scholarships are given to the participants who scored the highest marks.
  • Each task should be an individual submission and must be your own work.
  • Plagiarism is strictly prohibited; if found certificates will not be provided.

Hacktron Hackathon

General:

  • You should have to follow all the given instructions and guidelines.
  • Kindly remember that harassment, racism or inappropriate behaviour is not allowed during all the sessions.
  • The decision of the judge panel is the final decision. The decisions will be strictly in accordance to the rules and regulations of the Hackathon.

Hackathon:

  • Students of any higher educational institute are eligible to participate in the Hackathon.
  • The team should consist of 2 to 5 members. One person can only be on one team. If the judge panel identifies that someone is in two or more teams, all the contestants on the team will be disqualified.
  • It is compulsory for all the team members to be in the same university.
  • In the first round, all the teams should submit their ideas according to the given structure (Participants will be informed about it via email).
  • In the semi-finals, the selected thirty teams must present their ideas through a proposal and present to a judging panel (Participants will be informed about the structures via an email).
  • All the ten teams will be given two weeks to implement their game (If any team needs more time to implement, they can begin their implementation once submitting the proposal).
  • At the finals, the selected ten teams should demonstrate their game in front of a judge board (time for each team for demonstration - 10 min, Q&A - 5 min) and winners will be announced and awarded at the end of the session.
  • It is compulsory that all implementations should be their own work.
  • The implemented game can be 2D or 3D. Teams can use different effects and designs as their preferences.
  • Competitors are allowed to use libraries, frameworks, or open-source codes to make their projects. If the judge panel identified that there is any copyright issue all the contestants of the team will be disqualified.
  • To develop your game idea, you can use any kind of game engine platform.
  • When you submit your project make sure to submit your project file.
  • Each selected ten teams will be provided a mentor from the industry for their guidance.
  • Participants must treat all team members, competitors, judges, coaches, and volunteers with respect and courtesy while abiding by the Decoded code of conduct.
  • The decision on the winners of the competition will be made by the judges and it will be the final decision.
